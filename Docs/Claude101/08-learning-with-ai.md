# 08 — Learning with AI

> Part of the [PAL Second Brain](https://github.com/yourusername/pal-second-brain) documentation series.

---

## The Core Problem

> AI can make you significantly more productive OR less capable over time.
> The difference? How you use it.

**AI is your GPS.** Great for getting somewhere fast. Dangerous if you lose the ability to navigate without it. Truly useful when you understand the map AND use the GPS.

### The Skills Gap

```
Traditional learning: Problem → Struggle → Understanding → Solution
AI-assisted (wrong):  Problem → AI → Solution → ??? (no understanding)
AI-assisted (right):  Problem → Attempt → AI guidance → Understanding → Solution
```

The struggle isn't optional. It's where learning happens.

### Where AI Helps vs Where It Hurts

| High-Gain Tasks | Low/Negative-Gain Tasks |
|-----------------|-------------------------|
| Formatting and structuring notes | Deciding what matters to you |
| Processing inbox items at scale | Defining your personal goals |
| Finding patterns across domains | Creative judgment and priorities |
| Drafting initial content | Deep understanding of your own projects |
| Organizing large amounts of information | Building genuine expertise in a topic |

The pattern: **AI excels at well-defined, repeatable tasks**. It struggles with ambiguous problems requiring deep context or creative judgment.

## The Three Patterns

Every person using AI falls into one of three patterns:

| Pattern | Signs | Risk |
|---------|-------|------|
| **Dependent** | Can't start without AI, doesn't understand outputs, anxiety without it | Losing your own thinking ability |
| **Avoidant** | Refuses AI "on principle", slower than could be, dismissive of tools | Missing significant productivity gains |
| **Augmented** | Uses AI critically, understands everything, knows AI limits | Thriving |

### Pattern 1: Dependent

**The trap**: You delegate everything to AI. When it gets something wrong, you can't tell. You accept outputs without understanding them.

### Pattern 2: Avoidant

**The trap**: You refuse to use AI tools. You spend hours on tasks AI handles in minutes. You're not learning faster by doing everything manually — you're just slower.

### Pattern 3: Augmented

**The advantage**: You move fast AND understand deeply. You use AI for leverage, not replacement. You can work with OR without AI.

## The UVAL Protocol

A systematic approach to using AI without losing your edge.

| Step | Action | Why It Matters |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **U** | Understand First | Ask better questions, catch wrong answers |
| **V** | Verify | Ensure you actually learned, not just delegated |
| **A** | Apply | Transform knowledge into skill through modification |
| **L** | Learn | Capture insights for long-term retention |

### U — Understand First (The 15-Minute Rule)

Before asking AI for help:

1. **State the problem** in one sentence. If you can't, you don't understand it yet.
2. **Brainstorm 3 approaches**, even if you're unsure they'll work.
3. **Identify what you don't know** specifically.
4. **Then ask AI** — your question will be 10x better.

**Example:**

```
Bad:  "Organize my notes"
Good: "I have 30 unprocessed notes in my inbox. I want to:
       1. Add frontmatter with category and date
       2. Move research notes to PALProduct domain
       3. Move personal reflections to LifeOS domain
       I'm unsure how to categorize notes that span multiple domains."
```

### V — Verify (Explain It Back)

After AI generates output, can you explain:
- What was done and why?
- What would break if you changed a specific part?
- Why this approach instead of alternatives?

If you can't explain it, you haven't learned it.

### A — Apply (Transform, Don't Copy)

Never accept AI output without modification. Always do at least ONE:

1. **Adjust** — Change categories, labels, or structure to match your preferences
2. **Extend** — Add context the AI missed
3. **Simplify** — Remove parts you don't need
4. **Restructure** — Reorganize to fit your mental model

### L — Learn (Capture the Insight)

At the end of each session, capture ONE thing you learned. Not ten. One.

```markdown
## 2026-01-17
**Learned**: Notes that span multiple domains should go in the domain
where they'll be most useful, with a cross-reference link in the other.
**Context**: Had 5 notes touching both PALProduct and PALBuilder.
**Future me**: When in doubt, use the "primary audience" test — who
needs this information most?
```

## Using Claude Code for Learning

### CLAUDE.md Configuration for Learning Mode

Add to your `CLAUDE.md`:

```markdown
## Learning Mode
Before generating output for me, ask:
1. What approaches have I already considered?
2. What specifically am I stuck on?
3. What do I expect the result to look like?

If I skip these, remind me to think first.
```

### The 70/30 Weekly Split

| Activity | Time | AI Usage | Why |
|----------|------|----------|-----|
| **Learning new concepts** | 70% | 30% AI | Struggle builds understanding |
| **Applying known patterns** | 30% | 70% AI | Leverage what you already know |

**Example week:**

```
Monday:    Learn a new note-taking pattern  (minimal AI)
Tuesday:   Learn domain organization        (minimal AI)
Wednesday: Process inbox with AI            (full AI assistance)
Thursday:  Learn a new workflow             (minimal AI)
Friday:    Apply + organize                 (full AI assistance)
```

The key: Don't use AI heavily when learning NEW concepts. Use it heavily when applying concepts you already understand.

## 30-Day Progression Plan

### Week 1: Foundations

| Day | Activity | AI Usage |
|-----|----------|----------|
| 1-2 | Organize a small set of notes WITHOUT AI | 0% |
| 3 | Review: Can you explain your organization logic? | 0% |
| 4-5 | Redo the same task with AI review (not generation) | 20% |
| 6 | Set up your first domain manually | 0% |
| 7 | Rest/reflection | — |

### Week 2: Understanding

| Day | Activity | AI Usage |
|-----|----------|----------|
| 1-2 | Ask AI to process inbox, explain EVERY decision | 40% |
| 3 | Write your own INDEX.md, have AI review it | 30% |
| 4-5 | AI explains a skill workflow, you run it manually | 40% |
| 6 | Quiz yourself on the week's concepts | 10% |
| 7 | Rest/reflection | — |

### Week 3: Critical Usage

| Day | Activity | AI Usage |
|-----|----------|----------|
| 1-2 | Ask for multiple approaches to organizing a domain | 60% |
| 3 | Find errors in AI-suggested categorizations | 50% |
| 4-5 | Full workflow with AI assistance | 60% |
| 6 | Explain your entire system to someone (or a rubber duck) | 10% |
| 7 | Rest/reflection | — |

### Week 4: Augmented

| Day | Activity | AI Usage |
|-----|----------|----------|
| 1-5 | Real work with UVAL protocol | 70% |
| 6 | Review: What did you learn this week? | 10% |
| 7 | Plan next learning goals | — |

## Red Flags Checklist

| Red Flag | What's Happening | Immediate Action |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| Can't start without AI | Outsourced thinking | Organize something manually for 30 min |
| Don't understand AI's output | Accepting without learning | Use UVAL protocol on EVERYTHING |
| Can't explain your system | Surface-level usage | Describe your setup to someone |
| Anxiety without AI | Emotional dependence | It's a tool, not a lifeline — practice without |
| Every output looks the same | AI has patterns, you need variety | Study and organize manually |

### Weekly Self-Audit

Every Friday, ask:

1. What did I learn this week that I didn't know before?
2. Could I have done this week's work without AI?
3. Did I understand everything I produced?
4. Am I faster than last month? Am I more capable?

If you're faster but not more capable, you're building dependency.

## How PAL Uses This

PAL is designed to support the **Augmented** pattern. The system architecture encourages understanding through:

- **Spec-driven development:** Requirements are documented in plain language (Given-When-Then), so you always know WHY something works the way it does
- **Skills with workflows:** Instead of opaque automation, each skill has readable workflow files that explain each step
- **Session logging:** The session commands (`/sessions:session-start`, `/sessions:session-end`) capture what you learned and flag friction points for review

The UVAL Protocol maps directly to how PAL presents plans: Understand the intent, Verify against context, Apply with oversight, Log the result.
